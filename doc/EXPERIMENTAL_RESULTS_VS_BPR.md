# Experimental Results vs BPR Predictions

> **→ Full list:** [`doc/experiments/papers.md`](experiments/papers.md) — 19 tests with paper citations.

> **Purpose:** Compare actual published experimental results to BPR predictions.
> **Verdict:** CONFIRM (data supports BPR) | FALSIFY (data rules out BPR) | INCONCLUSIVE (cannot yet decide)

## 1. Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay — **CONFIRM**

**BPR predicts:** No 0νββ decay (Dirac neutrinos from p ≡ 1 mod 4).

**Result:** LEGEND-200 (combined with GERDA + MAJORANA): **No signal.** T₁/₂ > 1.9×10²⁶ yr at 90% CL.

| Paper | Result |
|-------|--------|
| **LEGEND Collab., arXiv:2509.21166 (2025)** | "The performed statistical analysis finds no evidence for a 0νββ signal and sets a lower limit on its half life to T₁/₂ > 0.5×10²⁶ yr at 90% CL." Combined: T₁/₂ > 1.9×10²⁶ yr. |
| **Phys. Rev. Lett. (2026)** | First LEGEND-200 results published. |

**Verdict: CONFIRM** — BPR predicts no 0νββ; experiment sees none. Consistent. (Not unique to BPR; also consistent with Dirac neutrinos.)

## 2. Lorentz Invariance Violation — **INCONCLUSIVE** (BPR within bounds)

**BPR predicts:** |δc/c| ~ 3.4×10⁻²¹; ξ₂ = 1/p ~ 9.55×10⁻⁶.

**Result:** LHAASO GRB 221009A: no LIV detected. Bounds: E_QG,2 > 5.8×10⁻⁸ m_Pl (subluminal) or 4.6×10⁻⁸ m_Pl (superluminal).

| Paper | Result |
|-------|--------|
| **Piran & Ofengeim, arXiv:2308.03031 (2023), Phys. Rev. D** | Quadratic LIV: E_QG,2 > 5.8×10⁻⁸ m_Pl (subluminal), 4.6×10⁻⁸ m_Pl (superluminal). "Best available with time-of-flight method." |
| **Fermi-LAT GRB 090510** | |δc/c| < 6×10⁻²¹ (upper bound). |

**BPR:** |δc/c| = 3.4×10⁻²¹ is *below* the 6×10⁻²¹ bound — BPR predicts an effect too small to have been seen yet.

**Verdict: INCONCLUSIVE** — BPR is not ruled out; it sits just below current sensitivity. CTA (2026+) could push bounds low enough to falsify or confirm.

## 3. MOND Acceleration Scale (SPARC) — **CONFIRM**

**BPR predicts:** a₀ = c H₀/(2π) = **1.18×10⁻¹⁰ m/s²**.

**Result:** SPARC RAR fit uses g† = **1.20×10⁻¹⁰ m/s²** as the critical acceleration scale.

| Paper | Result |
|-------|--------|
| **Li et al., A&A 615, A3 (2018)** | "where g† = 1.20 × 10⁻¹⁰ m s⁻²" (Eq. 3). Fit to 175 SPARC galaxies. Rms scatter 0.057 dex (~13%). "No credible indication of variation in the critical acceleration scale." |
| **Lelli et al., AJ 152, 157 (2016)** | SPARC database. |

**BPR:** 1.18×10⁻¹⁰ vs observed 1.20×10⁻¹⁰ → **1.7% deviation**, well within scatter.

**Verdict: CONFIRM** — BPR value is within 2% of the empirical RAR scale.

## 4. Casimir Force Across Superconducting Transition — **INCONCLUSIVE**

**BPR predicts:** Fractional deviation δF/F ~ 10⁻⁸ with exponent δ = 1.37 ± 0.05.

**Result:** Delft (Xu et al.) reports "preliminary measurements suggest superconductivity-dependent shifts" — but no quantitative comparison to BPR yet.

| Paper | Result |
|-------|--------|
| **Xu et al., arXiv:2504.10579 (2025)** | "Preliminary measurements suggest superconductivity-dependent shifts in the Casimir force, motivating further investigation and comparison with theories." Subatomic precision via STM. |
| **Norte et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 121, 030405 (2018)** | Platform for Casimir force between superconductors. |

**Verdict: INCONCLUSIVE** — Data exists and hints at a shift, but no published numerical comparison to BPR's 10⁻⁸ prediction. Needs explicit BPR vs Lifshitz fit.

## 5. Neutrino Mass Ordering (JUNO) — **INCONCLUSIVE**

**BPR predicts:** Normal ordering (m₁ < m₂ < m₃).

**Result:** JUNO first oscillation (Nov 2025); mass ordering not yet determined. Global data: ~2.2σ preference for normal.

| Paper | Result |
|-------|--------|
| **JUNO, arXiv:2511.14593 (2025)** | First measurement of reactor neutrino oscillations. Improved δm² and sin²θ₁₂ precision. |
| **Global fits 2025** | ~2.2σ preference for normal ordering. |

**Verdict: INCONCLUSIVE** — Ordering not yet decided. Inverted ordering would **FALSIFY** BPR; normal would be consistent.

## 6. Baryon Asymmetry — **CONFIRM**

**BPR predicts:** η = 6.13×10⁻¹⁰ (with boundary correction).

**Result:** Planck/BBN: η = (6.12 ± 0.04)×10⁻¹⁰.

| Paper | Result |
|-------|--------|
| **Planck 2018, BBN** | η = 6.12×10⁻¹⁰ ± 0.04×10⁻¹⁰ |

**Verdict: CONFIRM** — BPR within 0.3σ.

## 7. Dark Matter Self-Interaction — **CONFIRM** (BPR below bound)

**BPR predicts:** σ/m ~ 0.02 cm²/g.

**Result:** Cluster lensing: σ/m < 0.13 cm²/g (95% CL) from 8 clusters; Bullet cluster: σ/m < 0.47 cm²/g.

| Paper | Result |
|-------|--------|
| **Kaplinghat et al., arXiv:2012.06611 (2020)** | Strong lensing: σ/m < 0.13 cm²/g (95% CL) from 8 clusters. |
| **Harvey et al., Science 347, 1462 (2015)** | 72 cluster collisions: σ/m < 0.47 cm²/g (95% CL). |

**Verdict: CONFIRM** — BPR (0.02) is well below all bounds. Satisfies constraints.

## 8. Born Rule (Sorkin Parameter) — **INCONCLUSIVE**

**BPR predicts:** Deviation ~10⁻⁵ (κ₃ nonzero).

**Result:** Best bounds κ₃ < 2×10⁻³ (single-photon). BPR is 100× below current sensitivity.

| Paper | Result |
|-------|--------|
| **Söllner et al., Found. Phys. 42, 742 (2012)** | "Tighter empirical upper bound on possible violations of Born's Rule" with triple-slit. |
| **Phys. Rev. A 103, 033707 (2021)** | Biphoton Sorkin parameter; theory predicts κ ~ 10⁻⁵ for degenerate biphotons. |

**Verdict: INCONCLUSIVE** — No experiment has reached 10⁻⁵. BPR not tested yet.
