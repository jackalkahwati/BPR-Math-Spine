## 1. Casimir Force Across Superconducting Transition (Primary Falsifier)

**BPR predicts:** Fractional Casimir force deviation δF/F ~ 10⁻⁸ in the phonon-collective channel when measuring across a superconducting phase transition. The deviation has a specific functional form (Eq. 7) with exponent δ = 1.37 ± 0.05.

**Experiment:** Delft University – on-chip superconducting Casimir platform with STM readout.

| Reference | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Xu et al. (2025)** | *Measuring Casimir Force Across a Superconducting Transition*, arXiv:2504.10579. Microchip-based cavities with subatomic displacement resolution via STM. "Preliminary measurements suggest superconductivity-dependent shifts in the Casimir force." |
| **Phys. Rev. Lett. 121, 030405 (2018)** | *Platform for Measurements of the Casimir Force between Two Superconductors* – foundational Delft platform. |
| **Phys. Rev. A 99, 052507 (2019)** | *Casimir effect between superconductors* – Lifshitz theory for superconducting plates. |

**Confirm:** Anomalous Casimir shift near Tc at the predicted level (~10⁻⁸) with the predicted exponent.  
**Falsify:** Casimir force matches standard Lifshitz theory to 10⁻⁹ across the phase transition with no anomalous structure.
